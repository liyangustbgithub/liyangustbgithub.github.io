About

My research aims to make scientific and engineering computation faster, more scalable, and more reliable. I am interested in (i) evolutionary and hybrid optimization for model calibration and scientific ML, (ii) performance engineering on modern heterogeneous systems (CPU/GPU and domestic supercomputer architectures), and (iii) physics-informed learning for PDEs with improved robustness and uncertainty awareness.

Research Themes

  • EC for Scientific Computing: parallel evolutionary optimization, hybrid EC+gradient strategies, multi-objective accuracy–cost trade-offs.
  • HPC & Performance Engineering: scalable solvers and kernels, sparse linear algebra (SpMV/Krylov/AMG), architecture-aware optimization.
  • PINNs & Trustworthy PDE Solvers: sampling/training stability, uncertainty-aware modeling, coupling PINNs with numerical solvers.

Research

1) Evolutionary Computation (EC)

  • Parallel EC algorithms (genetic algorithms, swarm intelligence) for parameter calibration and inverse problems.
  • Hybrid EC + gradient-based optimization and surrogate modeling for robust, data-efficient learning.
  • Multi-objective optimization for accuracy–runtime–memory trade-offs in simulation and PINN training.

2) High-Performance Computing (HPC)

  • Performance engineering for heterogeneous and domestic supercomputer architectures (CPU/GPU and many-core systems).
  • Scalable solver stacks: Krylov methods, AMG preconditioning, and sparse kernels such as SpMV.
  • Reproducible benchmarking and large-scale parallel performance evaluation.

3) Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINN)

  • Training point sampling strategies (with/without analytical solutions) and curriculum design for improved convergence.
  • Uncertainty-aware PINNs and approaches to improve confidence in CFD/reactor simulations.
  • Hybrid PINN–numerical methods for reliability and speed in PDE solution pipelines.
